April 16, 2020: Here are some of the stories we've been following today.
Majority of Montreal CHSLDs have outbreaks
Three-quarters of Montreal CHSLDs, or long-term care centres, have COVID-19 outbreaks, while 23 per cent of private seniors' homes have cases, regional public health director Mylène Drouin said Thursday. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7slz ).
Confusion, frustration as restrictions eased on caregivers
Thursday was supposed to be the first day caregivers were permitted to return to long-term care facilities and seniors' homes. Instead, confusion reigned about the criteria required to permit caregivers into the facilities, and the province was forced to clarify the rules it laid out less than 24 hours earlier. Read more( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/confusion-frustration-reign-as-quebec-loosens-restrictions-on-caregivers/ ).
True death toll from COVID-19?
The full extent of the coronavirus death toll won't become clear for many months, experts say, because a lack of widespread testing and blind spots in the way deaths are counted hinder a thorough body count. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7shB ).

Analysis: Why is Quebec the COVID-19 epicentre of Canada?
The question was bound to come up, and there are no simple answers yet. With Quebec registering its biggest daily increase in COVID-19( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/coronavirus-live-updates-do-more-testing-in-seniors-residences-families-say/wcm/34e369be-4952-4bec-b9fa-4ba0459d1ebc/ ) mortalities Thursday and officials scrambling to rescue the elderly by parachuting more workers into seniors' residences, questions about who is to blame for the disaster in those facilities and what Quebec's level of preparation was are starting to emerge. Food services for seniors in N.D.G., Lachine
When Constance Lafontaine recently spoke to a woman in her 90s who was preparing to go grocery shopping, she knew action was required. Concordia University, with help from the CIUSSS du Centre-Ouest-de-l'Île-de-Montréal and New Hope Seniors Centre, initiated COVID-19 Grocery Response N.D.G., a grocery-ordering and delivery program for seniors 70 and over who are being told to self-isolate due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The program is now in its third week. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7sjT ).
Cancer treatments disrupted by pandemic: poll
Nearly two-thirds of Quebec cancer patients have seen their treatments disrupted because of the coronavirus crisis( http://montrealgazette.com/tag/coronavirus ), a poll suggests. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7seP ).
New Caisse CEO rejigs structure, appoints executives
The Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec is making a series of executive appointments as newly installed boss Charles Emond puts his stamp on the public pension fund manager. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7snm ).
Money-making Loto-Québec, Hydro-Québec, SAQ shaken
A drop in electricity consumption, shuttered casinos and provincial liquor stores closed on Sundays while Quebec's finances continue to be stretched by the COVID-19 crisis. The way things are going, there's a risk the Legault government will have to revise just how much cash its money-making Crown corporations can be relied upon to send to Quebec's coffers. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7sjD ).
Medical gown order a 'lifeline' for textile company
A Montreal textile manufacturer is recalling two-thirds of its workforce thanks to a government contract that will allow it to branch out into medical clothing. Notre-Dame-de-Grâce's Tricots Liesse plans to bring back about 100 laid-off workers next week after winning a contract Wednesday to make gowns for the Quebec Health Ministry, owner and president Claude Helwani said Thursday. Read more( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-7sjZ ).
