Public-health officials in Quebec say that 33 people have died because of the week-long heat wave in the province.
They say 18 of those deaths were on the island of Montreal.
The temperature in Montreal was expected to reach a high of 35 degrees Celsius on Thursday with a humidex value of 45 C, according to Environment Canada.
The heat wave is expected to break overnight Thursday to Friday.
Meanwhile, Environment Canada issued a smog warning( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/smog-warning-issued-for-montreal-area-as-heat-wave-enters-last-gasp ) and maintained its heat warning for the Montreal area and recommends individuals drink six to eight glasses of water par day, avoid alcoholic beverages, take at least one shower or cool bath per day and cool their skin several times per day with wet towels and limit physical activities.
