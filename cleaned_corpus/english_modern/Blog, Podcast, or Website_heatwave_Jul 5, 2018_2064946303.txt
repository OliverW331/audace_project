The city of Pointe-Claire is taking steps to help citizens cope with the heat wave( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/heat-wave-33-quebecers-have-died-18-in-montreal-health-officials-say ) in Montreal.
The city is notifying residents( https://www.pointe-claire.ca/en/news/ ) about air-conditioned municipal buildings open to the public during regular hours:
Aquatic Centre (60 Maywood Ave.): 5:30 a.m. to 9:30 p.m., Monday to Friday.
Central Library (100 Douglas-Shand Ave.): 10 a.m. to 9 p.m, Monday to Friday.
Valois Library Branch (68 Prince-Edward Ave.): 1 to 9 p.m., Wednesday and Friday.
Bob-Birnie Arena (58 Maywood Ave.): 7 a.m. to midnight, every day.
The Aquatic Centre has extended its family swim hours. Until Thursday, July 5: 1:30 to 9:30 p.m., in the 50-m (six-lane) pool.
Admission is free for residents with a MULTI Card.
There will also be extended opening hours until Friday, July 6, at the Valois Park splash pad, at 40 Baie-de-Valois Ave. The splash pad will be open from 7 a.m. to 10:30 p.m.
The city's eco-grazing sheep at Edgewater Park will stay at the farm.
Thursday evening's outdoor activity will take place indoors at 6:30 p.m. at the NoÃ«l-Legault Community Centre, 245 Lakeshore Rd.
Other recommendations for staying cool include taking a cool bath or shower, spending a few hours in an air-conditioned shopping mall or by reducing physical activities.
Also remember to drink plenty of water and wear light clothing.
