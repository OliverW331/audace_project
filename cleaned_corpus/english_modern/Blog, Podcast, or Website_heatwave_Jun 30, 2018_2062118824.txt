Indoors:
Kamasi Washington( https://www.montrealjazzfest.com/en-CA/Programmation/Concert/9452 ) (8:30 p.m. Metropolis, $49 to $70)
This L.A. saxophone visionary has been ushering in a new era of jazz since the release of his aptly titled 2015 double-album The Epic. His new EP, The Choice, shows more of the sweeping grandeur and creativity that has seen him sit in with everyone from Kendrick Lamar to Herby Hancock.
Outdoors:
Spanish Harlem Orchestra( https://www.montrealjazzfest.com/en/Programmation/Concert/9665 ) (9 and 11 p.m., TD Stage, corner Jeanne Mance St. and de Maisonneuve Blvd., free)
There will be dancing in the streets as this venerable New York/Nuyorican salsa ensemble brings the fire to the outdoor main stage. And they're calling for a heat wave( http://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/montreal-prepares-for-hottest-recorded-canada-day-as-heat-wave-rolls-in )? Of course they are.
Ghost Note feat. MonoNeon( https://www.montrealjazzfest.com/en/Programmation/Concert/9671 ) (10 p.m., Club Jazz Casino de Montreal, corner Bleury St. and René-Lévesque Blvd., free)
Two nights after rocking Metropolis with funky fusion jazz band Snarky Puppy, drummer Robert "Sput" Searight and percussionist Nate Werth present their new project Ghost Note, accompanied by bass fiend MonoNeon, aka Dywane Thomas Jr., who was among the last people to play with Prince.
The Montreal International Jazz Festival continues to July 7. For more information, visit montrealjazzfest.com( http://montrealjazzfest.com/ )
