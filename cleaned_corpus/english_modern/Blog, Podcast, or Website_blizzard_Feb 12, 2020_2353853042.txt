The snow removal operation launched to clear 10,000 kilometres of streets and sidewalks in Montreal in the wake of a storm that on Friday( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/schools-out-plows-hit-the-roads-as-montreal-braces-for-50-cm-of-snow ) dumped 30 centimetres of snow on the city stood at 46 per cent on Tuesday evening.
According to Montreal's snow-removal monitoring sites( https://montreal.ca/en/snow-removal ), only four of the 19 boroughs - Plateau-Mont-Royal, Montreal North, Anjou and Lachine - had managed to pass the 50 per cent mark by 9:30 p.m.

The operation began on Saturday and is expected to continue for at least a week, the city's original deadline of Friday disappearing under an additional 15 centimetres of snow( https://montrealgazette.com/news/local-news/montreal-city-crews-snowed-under-by-mondays-surprise-15-cm ) that fell on Monday while another 10 centimetres are expected Thursday.
The city is urging motorists not to park their vehicles at an angle on the street and to respect no parking signs to ensure the snow removal progresses efficiently.
