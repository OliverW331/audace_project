About 50 flights scheduled to leave Montreal-Trudeau airport in Montreal were cancelled Monday morning in anticipation of a major snowfall here and freezing rain and snowstorms elsewhere along the Eastern Seaboard.
Meanwhile, Jean-Lesage airport in Quebec City had cancelled four flights.
The cancellations are the first signs of a rough day in store for commuters and travellers, as 15 to 25 centimetres of snow are expected to fall between Monday afternoon and Tuesday night in the Montreal and Quebec City areas.
By 10:30 a.m. the Sûreté du Québec was calling for caution on area highways after freezing rain began to fall over the Montreal area, urging motorists to adapt their driving to the road conditions.
About 90 minutes after that warning from provincial police, that precipitation was followed by snow and the freezing ran warning lifted.
Transport Quebec was reporting that road surfaces were beginning be covered on Highways 15 and 20 on the island of Montreal, Highways 13, 15 and 440 in Laval and on Highways 10, 15, 30, 132 and 138 on the South Shore.
Environment Canada also warns( https://weather.gc.ca/warnings/report_e.html?qc67#1911592041984294974201912290504ww1070cwul ) that strong wind gusts will also be a part of Montreal's weather cocktail, potentially causing white outs and reducing visibility on local roads and highways.
At 9 a.m., Environment Canada added a freezing rain warning( https://weather.gc.ca/warnings/report_e.html?qc67#1834404071984294974201912290504ww1070cwul ) to the snowfall warning it had already issued for the Montreal area.
Monday's forecast( https://wp.me/p4Uzqt-747p ) calls for cloudy skies with snow at times mixed with ice pellets beginning in the morning morning. There is a risk of freezing rain and a 10 cm. accumulation of snow and ice pellets. The forecast high of minus 2 C. and a wind chill of minus 12.
This evening's forecast calls for another 10 cm. of snow, a temperature of minus 2 C. and a wind chill of minus 9 C.
