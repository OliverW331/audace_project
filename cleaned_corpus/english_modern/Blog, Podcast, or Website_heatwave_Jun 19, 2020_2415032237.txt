The Environment Canada heat warning in effect for Montreal and southwest and central Quebec has been extended from Sunday to Tuesday, with temperatures in many areas expected to climb to 35 Celsius.
The combination of high temperatures and humidity will create humidex factors as high as 40 C, particularly in heavily urbanized areas such as Montreal.
The city of Montreal has posted a map detailing the locations of water parks, pools and air-conditioned installations on its territory. It has also established a webpage on how to cool off over the course of the summer.

Environment Canada as well as the Quebec Health Ministry warn that your physical condition can deteriorate during heat waves and recommend that you:
- Drink 6 to 8 glasses of water per day. Follow your physician's instructions regarding the amount of fluid to drink.
- Avoid alcoholic beverages.
- If you can, spend at least two hours a day in an air-conditioned or cool location.
- Take at least one cool shower or bath per day, or cool your skin several times per day with wet towels.
- Limit physical activity.
- Wear light clothes.
